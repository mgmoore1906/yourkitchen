SMS Opt-In & Consent

How users consent to receive text messages from YourKitchen.

YourKitchen LLC operates a meal coordination platform. This page is the complete, publicly accessible record of our SMS program: how end users provide express written consent, the exact disclosures shown at the point of opt-in, and a screenshot of the actual consent screen. No login is required to review any of this information.

For compliance reviewers: the consent screen shown below is the exact first step every user completes during signup. The screenshot, consent language, and opt-in steps on this page fully document the opt-in flow — no account or login is needed to verify it.

How users opt in

YourKitchen collects SMS consent through two opt-in paths, depending on whether the person is a meal recipient or someone sending a meal (a coordinator). In both, the consent checkbox is unchecked by default, must be actively checked, and the action button stays disabled until it is. Consent is never pre-checked or assumed. The screenshots below show exactly what each user sees.

1. Recipient opt-in (account signup)

When a recipient creates their YourKitchen account, the first step of onboarding presents a phone-number field and the SMS consent checkbox shown here:

Recipient signup (Step 1 of 6). The phone field notes it is used only for Y/N meal confirmations via SMS, and the consent checkbox below it carries the full consent language with STOP/HELP. The box is unchecked by default.

2. Coordinator opt-in (sending a meal)

When someone sends a meal to a recipient, the final checkout step presents their phone-number field and the same SMS consent checkbox shown here:

Coordinator checkout. The consent checkbox carries the full consent language with STOP/HELP and links to the Privacy Policy and Terms. The box is unchecked by default and must be checked to proceed.

Exact consent language shown at opt-in

✓
I agree to receive recurring SMS text messages from YourKitchen, including meal proposals, confirmations, and delivery updates. Message frequency varies by activity. Message & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out or HELP for help. See our Terms and Privacy Policy.

The opt-in steps

Both paths follow the same pattern:

1
User reaches the signup step (recipient) or the meal-checkout step (coordinator) in the YourKitchen app.
2
User enters their name and mobile phone number.
3
User actively checks the consent box shown above. The box is unchecked by default.
4
User taps the action button (disabled until the box is checked) to complete opt-in.

What messages we send

  • Meal proposals — when someone wants to send the user a meal, requiring a Y/N reply to confirm.
  • Confirmations — when a meal is confirmed and dispatched.
  • Delivery updates — when a meal is on the way or delivered.

Message frequency varies based on how often the user receives meal offers. These are transactional, conversational messages — not marketing.

Sample messages

YourKitchen: Marques wants to send you dinner on Tue, Jun 10 — Pad Thai from Up Thai Kitchen. Reply Y to confirm or N to decline. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.
YourKitchen: Your meal is confirmed! Peri-Peri Chicken from Peli Peli Kitchen is on its way. Track: app.yourkitchen.app/track/4821. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.
YourKitchen: Your meal was delivered. Thank you for using YourKitchen. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.

Opt-out & help

Users can reply STOP at any time to immediately unsubscribe from all messages. Replying HELP returns support contact information. These keywords are honored automatically.

Consent is never shared

Mobile opt-in consent and phone numbers are never shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing purposes. Phone numbers are used solely to operate the YourKitchen meal coordination service.
