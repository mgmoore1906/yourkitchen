How it works

Three steps.
One less thing to worry about.

1

Someone creates a Kitchen

A friend, family member, or the recipient sets up a YourKitchen calendar — choosing how many months of support they need, their meal preferences, allergies, and preferred delivery window.

5 minutes to set up
2

The village claims dates

Friends and family get a link to the calendar. They pick a date, choose a meal from a curated local restaurant list, leave a personal note — and save their card. No awkward payment requests. No forgotten commitments.

One tap to claim a date
3

The recipient confirms and eats

On the day, the recipient gets a text: "Marcus picked dinner for tonight — does this work?" They reply Y to confirm or swap to something else. The order is placed automatically. Dinner arrives.

Y to confirm · N to swap

The basics

YourKitchen is a meal coordination platform that lets the people who love you send dinner from restaurants you actually want — without any awkward asking, group texts, or duplicate deliveries. You set up a Kitchen with your preferred restaurants and open calendar dates. Your village gets a link, claims a date, picks a meal, and dinner shows up. You just reply Y to confirm.
Anyone whose village wants to show up but doesn't have a coordinated way to do it. That includes new parents navigating life with a newborn, families where a loved one is in treatment, spouses holding down the home while a partner is deployed, and families processing grief. If life just got heavier and dinner feels like one more thing — YourKitchen is for you.
Traditional meal trains rely on someone manually coordinating who's bringing what, managing dietary restrictions over group texts, and hoping people follow through. YourKitchen automates all of that — restaurants are pre-approved, delivery is handled by DoorDash, and you confirm each meal with a single text reply. No home cooking logistics, no drop-off coordination, no three lasagnas on the same Tuesday.

Once the Kitchen is live, they just respond to SMS confirmations. No app downloads required.

Delivery & restaurants

All deliveries are handled by DoorDash. When a recipient confirms a meal with a Y reply, the order is placed automatically and a Dasher is assigned. Both the recipient and coordinator receive an SMS with a live DoorDash tracking link. No separate app needed — everything happens through YourKitchen.
At launch, YourKitchen offers a curated list of restaurants in each supported city — a mix of popular local favorites and well-known chains, all available for DoorDash delivery. The recipient selects their preferred restaurants during Kitchen setup, so coordinators always order from a pre-approved list. We're launching in Houston, Indianapolis, Atlanta, Dallas, Chicago, Washington D.C., Los Angeles, and New York. More cities coming soon.
You can request any restaurant directly from the My Restaurants page inside your Kitchen. Fill out a quick form — restaurant name, city, and any notes — and we'll add it within 24 hours. We personally curate every restaurant to make sure it's available on DoorDash and delivers to your address.
The morning of the delivery date — not when the coordinator books it. If a coordinator books a meal two weeks out, the recipient won't hear about it until the day arrives. Notification timing is meal-type aware: breakfast slots notify at 6:30 AM, lunch at 9:00 AM, and dinner at 2:00 PM. This gives recipients enough time to confirm before delivery.
They reply N to decline. The coordinator is notified — no charge is applied — and the date reopens for anyone else in the village to claim. The recipient can optionally send a short note to the coordinator explaining why, so they can try a different date.
At launch we're serving Houston, Indianapolis, Atlanta, Dallas, Chicago, Washington D.C., Los Angeles, and New York. If you're outside these cities, join the waitlist and we'll let you know when we expand to your area. Coordinators can be anywhere in the country — geo only applies to where the meals are delivered.

Payments & pricing

Only after the recipient confirms. Coordinators enter their payment info when submitting a proposal, but nothing is charged until the recipient replies Y. If the recipient declines or the proposal expires, no charge is applied — ever.
YourKitchen charges a 3% platform fee on each confirmed order, regardless of which plan the recipient is on. This covers the coordination infrastructure, SMS notifications, and DoorDash integration. Standard DoorDash delivery fees and taxes also apply, just as they would on any DoorDash order.
YourKitchen is free to set up and use. The Free plan gives you a 60-day calendar, up to 3 restaurants, and in-app notifications — no card required. Every account also includes a 14-day full Care+ trial so you can experience everything before deciding. Care+ is $9.99/month and adds SMS confirmations, a 12-month calendar, and up to 10 restaurants. An Early Adopter annual plan is available at $59/year (about 50% off monthly), and a Founding Member lifetime plan is available for $149 — limited to the first 100 members.
Founding Member is a one-time $149 payment for lifetime access — price never increases, even as we raise rates. It includes everything in Care+, unlimited active Kitchens, direct access to the founder, voting rights on new features before they ship, early beta access, and your name listed in YourKitchen's founding story on the about page. Limited to the first 100 members.

Privacy & safety

Your Kitchen link is not publicly indexed — it's only discoverable by people you share it with. That said, anyone with the link can view your Kitchen and submit a meal proposal. We recommend sharing it only with people you trust. In a future update, you'll be able to require an invite code for additional privacy.
No. Coordinators never see your home address. When a coordinator submits a proposal, the order is placed by YourKitchen directly with DoorDash using your address on file. The coordinator only sees that the order was placed and receives a confirmation once it's delivered.
All payments are processed by Stripe, one of the world's most trusted payment platforms. YourKitchen never stores card numbers. Stripe handles everything — card capture, authorization, and charging — only when a recipient confirms a meal.
