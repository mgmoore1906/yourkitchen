Why we built this

When life got heavy,
her village wanted to help.

YourKitchen started in our own home in Waller, Texas — three kids under five, and a community that kept asking the same question: "What can I do?" The honest answer was almost always dinner. But meal trains fall apart in group texts, double up on the same night, and quietly fade. We wanted something that just worked.

"I didn't have to ask. I didn't have to organize anything. People I loved just… showed up, one dinner at a time. That was everything."

How it works

Three steps.
One less thing to worry about.

1

Someone creates a Kitchen

A friend, family member, or the recipient sets up a calendar — choosing how long they need support, meal preferences, allergies, and a delivery window.

5 minutes to set up
2

The village claims dates

Friends and family get a link. They pick a date, choose a meal from a curated local restaurant list, leave a note, and save their card. No awkward payment asks.

One tap to claim a date
3

The recipient confirms and eats

On the day, the recipient gets a text: "Marques picked dinner for tonight — does this work?" They reply Y to confirm or swap. Dinner arrives.

Y to confirm · N to swap
